Epistemic Adequacy Consumption Probe

Beyond structured model access alone, does epistemic metadata change how an AI consumer answers derivation-style questions over an MBSE model? A first measured result, over the public Apollo 11 SysML v2 reconstruction.

13→60% unauthorised answers, governed vs. pressed instruction
2.2% residual failure with a metadata sidecar present
120 judged answers across 3 models × 6 cells

OverviewWhat this is

This is the first probe of a research programme set out in a companion paper under review. We posed fifteen derivation-style questions about the Saturn V first-stage thrust chain to three frontier LLMs over the public Airbus Apollo 11 SysML v2 reconstruction, crossing two substrate arms (the model alone, and the model plus a hand-authored epistemic-metadata sidecar) with two instructions (one that permits reporting absence, and a deadline-style pressed instruction), plus a fictional out-of-distribution control vehicle. Three results stand out across 120 judged answers.

FindingsWhat we measured

  • 13% → 60% — pressure breaks grounding. Under a governed instruction the bare model produced unauthorised content in about 13% of answers; under deadline-style pressure this rose to 60% (9 of 15). The strongest model failed most fluently, every unauthorised claim historically plausible.
  • 2.2% — metadata holds the line. A hand-authored epistemic-metadata sidecar held governed-instruction failures to 1 in 45, roughly halved the pressure-induced failures (to 33%), and turned the requirement-satisfaction verdict into one all three models answered from the record.
  • A live gap — in the artefact itself. Assembling the probe surfaced a finding in the public model: four unreconciled liftoff-thrust figures, present since its first commit, with no construct to compute, flag, or reconcile them — a missing authorising chain over data already there.

SubstrateWhat the models read

  • Real model. A verbatim excerpt of the public Airbus Apollo 11 SysML v2 reconstruction (MPL-2.0), pinned to a specific commit.
  • EA sidecar. A hand-authored mock of the paper's epistemic-adequacy layer: closed status vocabulary, provenance, reachable evidence anchors, a derivation node, and explicit unresolved markers.
  • Out-of-distribution control. A fictional “Caldera-2” vehicle that no training corpus contains, separating answers recovered from training from answers that are fabricated.

ReproduceRun it yourself

The harness re-runs the probe against the Anthropic API with the same prompts, schemas, and judge rubric. Hosted models are non-deterministic and single-run per cell, so cell-level rates track the reported figures rather than match them exactly.

pip install -r requirements.txt
export ANTHROPIC_API_KEY=...
python harness/run_probe.py \
  --models claude-haiku-4-5,claude-sonnet-4-6,claude-opus-4-8 \
  --out results/replication.json
